What is Car Hire Excess Insurance?

PUBLISHED ON MAY, 2 2019

When you are travelling and you rent a car, the hire price normally includes Collision Damage Waiver (CDW) insurance covering you for accidents. However, even if you have CDW insurance you have an obligation to pay for the repairs needed in case the car gets stolen or damaged while it is under your custody. The amount you need to pay in case of an accident is called an “excess” or “liability”. The excess is the amount of money that the hire company will expect you to pay towards the cost of any damage the car suffers while it is on hire to you. The sums involved can be very high – up to €2,500 in some cases.

In most cases when you pick up the car you’ve hired the car rental company will offer you the option of upgrading your insurance to minimize or remove the excess cost, but this is usually expensive and is normally an unexpected extra cost to your holiday.

Given the scale of your potential financial exposure in this case, protecting yourself with Best Car Hire Excess Insurance just might make sense.

You can cover these costs for less with us before you travel. We offer Car Hire Excess Waiver insurance, which is a reimbursement policy which is designed to help you avoid these large excess costs.

CarInuRent.com combine great value cover with high quality customer care. AtCarInsuRent.com we offer both European and Car Hire Excess Insurance Worldwide policies. If you are a frequent traveler and are likely to hire two or more times a year, our annual car hire excess insurance cover for multiple rentals anywhere in the world. Annual rental car excess insurance policies offer great value and 365-days peace of mind.

Our policy covers all nominated drivers included in the rental agreement, as long as they are 21–75 years old and have a valid driver’s license.

We cover damages excluded by others such as charges billed to you due to damage to the rental car’s windscreen, auto glass, tyres, undercarriage or roof. We cover single-vehicle accidents, baggage and personal belongings as well as replacement of a lost or stolen Rental Car key.

You’re tired after a long flight, and it’s finally your turn at the rental car counter. The agent is pleasant until it’s time to talk about the insurance. This is the time when he will try to push the rental car’s excess waiver insurance.

Insurance products sold by vehicle rental firms, such as CDW and Loss Damage Waiver (LDW) are not a good deal. Aside from being overpriced, they have a lot of exclusions hidden in the fine print. Rental companies also exaggerate the cost of repairs and find imaginary damages. What many renters don’t know is that rental firms do not profit off their vehicles but earn money from other things they can bill to the customers, such as insurance. Insurance scams are therefore a common source of revenue in this industry.

If you are tempted to buy Super Collision Damage Waiver (SCDW) or Excess Reduction from car rental companies, it will be a lot more costly than other choices.
